3D Time-Dependent Schrödinger Equation for a Particle in a Box

Problem Statement:

Consider a particle of mass $m$ confined within a cubic box of side length $L$ ($0 \le x, y, z \le L$) with an infinite potential at the boundaries and zero potential inside. The time evolution of the particle's wave function $\Psi(x, y, z, t)$ is governed by the 3D time-dependent Schrödinger equation:

$i \hbar \frac{\partial \Psi}{\partial t} = -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \nabla^2 \Psi$

with the following boundary conditions:

  • $\Psi = 0$ at $x = 0, L$
  • $\Psi = 0$ at $y = 0, L$
  • $\Psi = 0$ at $z = 0, L$

and the initial condition at $t = 0$:

$\Psi(x, y, z, 0) = A' \sin\left(\frac{\pi x}{L}\right) \sin\left(\frac{\pi y}{L}\right) \sin\left(\frac{\pi z}{L}\right) + B' \sin\left(\frac{2\pi x}{L}\right) \sin\left(\frac{\pi y}{L}\right) \sin\left(\frac{\pi z}{L}\right)$

where $A'$ and $B'$ are normalization constants.

Solution:

The solution to this problem is given by the superposition of two stationary states that match the initial condition:

$\Psi(x, y, z, t) = A' \sin\left(\frac{\pi x}{L}\right) \sin\left(\frac{\pi y}{L}\right) \sin\left(\frac{\pi z}{L}\right) e^{-i E_{1, 1, 1} t / \hbar} + B' \sin\left(\frac{2\pi x}{L}\right) \sin\left(\frac{\pi y}{L}\right) \sin\left(\frac{\pi z}{L}\right) e^{-i E_{2, 1, 1} t / \hbar}$

where the energy eigenvalues are:

$E_{n_x, n_y, n_z} = \frac{\hbar^2 \pi^2}{2m L^2} (n_x^2 + n_y^2 + n_z^2)$

Specifically, for the two terms in the solution:

$E_{1, 1, 1} = \frac{3 \hbar^2 \pi^2}{2m L^2}$
$E_{2, 1, 1} = \frac{6 \hbar^2 \pi^2}{2m L^2}$

This solution satisfies the Schrödinger equation, the boundary conditions (being zero at the walls of the box due to the sine functions), and the initial condition at $t = 0$ (where the exponential terms become unity).
