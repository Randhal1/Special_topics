1D Time-Dependent Schrödinger Equation
Infinite Square Well

Equation

$$ i\hbar\,\frac{\partial \psi(x,t)}{\partial t} \;=\; -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\,\frac{\partial^2 \psi(x,t)}{\partial x^2} \quad (0 < x < L) $$

Boundary & Initial Conditions

  • $\psi(0,t)=0,\;\psi(L,t)=0$
  • At $t=0$: $\psi(x,0)=A\,\sin\bigl(\tfrac{n\pi x}{L}\bigr)$

Exact Solution

$$ \psi(x,t) = A\, \sin\!\Bigl(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\Bigr) \,\exp\!\Bigl(-\,\frac{i\,E_n\,t}{\hbar}\Bigr) $$

with energy levels

$$ E_n = \frac{n^2\pi^2\hbar^2}{2m\,L^2} ,\quad n=1,2,3,\dots $$

This solution satisfies the PDE, vanishes at the walls, and matches the initial sine‐mode at $t=0$.
